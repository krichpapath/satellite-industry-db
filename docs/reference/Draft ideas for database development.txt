An industrial database is not just a list of firms. It is a strategic intelligence system that integrates:
Firm-level information
Industrial structural data
Technology/Innovation data
Human capital data
Supply Chain & Ecosystem Mapping
Investment & Financial Indicators
Sustainability indicators
When designed properly, it becomes the backbone for:
Industrial upgrading
Technology roadmapping implementation
STI policy design
Cluster development
Investment targeting
Creating an industrial database requires structured, multi-dimensional information that supports policy planning, industrial development, technology roadmapping, investment decisions, and ecosystem coordination.

1️⃣ Firm-Level Information (Core Layer)
A. Basic Company Profile
Company name
Registration number
Year established
Ownership structure (local / foreign / JV)
Parent company (if any)
Contact information
Industrial classification (e.g., ISIC, NAICS)
Geographic location (province, industrial zone, cluster)
B. Size & Capacity
Number of employees
Revenue / turnover
Production capacity
Export percentage
Main markets (domestic/export regions)

2️⃣ Industrial Structure Information
A. Value Chain Position
Upstream (raw materials)
Midstream (components/sub-assembly)
Downstream (final assembly)
Aftermarket/service
B. Product & Service Portfolio
Main products
Technology intensity (low/medium/high-tech)
Standard certifications (ISO, automotive grade, etc.)
Key customers / OEM linkages

3️⃣ Technology & Innovation Data 
A. Technology Capability
Core technologies mastered
Patents (number, field)
R&D expenditure
R&D personnel
Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
Digitalization level (Industry 4.0 adoption)
B. Infrastructure
Testing facilities
Lab capability
Manufacturing processes
Software/hardware integration capability
C. Innovation Collaboration
University partnerships
Public research institute collaborations
Participation in joint R&D programs
Membership in industrial associations

4️⃣ Human Resource & Skill Data
Workforce skill categories
Engineers / technicians ratio
Specialized skills (e.g., battery engineering, AI, embedded systems)
Training programs participated in
Skill gaps identified

5️⃣ Supply Chain & Ecosystem Mapping
To enable ecosystem analysis and SNA (Social Network Analysis):
Key suppliers
Key buyers
Strategic partners
Membership in clusters
Participation in government programs
Network density (optional advanced layer)

6️⃣ Investment & Financial Indicators
Capital investment trends
Access to funding
Government incentives received
Offset agreements (if relevant)
Export/import dependency

7️⃣ Sustainability & ESG Indicators 
Energy consumption
Renewable energy usage
Carbon footprint
Waste management system
ESG certification


An effective industrial database should allow:
Firm-level drill-down
Sector-level aggregation
Value-chain mapping
Technology capability heatmap
Gap analysis functionality
Dashboard visualization
Periodic updating mechanism
